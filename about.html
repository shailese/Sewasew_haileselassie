Dr. Sewasew Haileselassie Assefa is a collections and educational programming manager at The Griot Museum of Black History. Her current work focuses on developing archival collections to facilitate community access. Her current archaeological research in the East African Coast investigates sociopolitical and economic transformations and their impact on ancient populations. Her aim is to understand how changes to the environment, governance, and population movement affect food habits and health outcomes. Her research has been funded by the National Science Foundation (2023 - 2025) and Wenner-Gren Foundation (2023-2025). Her research integrates multiple archaeological approches including bioarchaeology, biomolecular methods, archival research, and community-centered research.
