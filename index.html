Dr. Sewasew Haileselassie Assefa is an archaeologist studying diet and health in ancient East Africa. Her archaeological research investigates sociopolitical and economic transformations and their impact on ancient populations. Her aim is to understand how changes to the environment, governance, and population movement affect food habits and health outcomes. Her research integrates multiple archaeological approches including bioarchaeology, biomolecular methods, archival research, and community-centered research. Her research has been funded by the National Science Foundation (2023 - 2025) and Wenner-Gren Foundation (2023-2025). Currently, she is a collections and educational programming manager at The Griot Museum of Black History. At the Griot Museum, she focuses on developing archival collections to facilitate community access.
